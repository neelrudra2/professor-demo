Research Facility

Major instruments, sample capabilities and brief descriptions

Electrochemical Workstation (Potentiostat)

Used to run electrochemical experiments by precisely controlling and measuring voltage and current on electrodes — essential for battery, corrosion, and sensor research.

Vacuum Oven

A controlled oven that operates under reduced pressure to gently remove solvents and moisture at lower temperatures — useful for drying sensitive samples and solvent removal.

Source Measurement Unit (SMU)

A precision electronic instrument that can source voltage or current and simultaneously measure voltage/current — commonly used for I–V characterization of devices and materials.

Centrifuge

Spins liquid samples at high speed to separate components by density — widely used for sample clarification, cell pelleting and separating phases.

Horn Ultrasonicator (Sonicator)

Applies high-frequency ultrasonic waves (via a probe/horn) to mix, disperse or lyse samples — commonly used for cell disruption, emulsification and nanoparticle dispersion.

Wet Lab (benches & basic utilities)

A dedicated space with sinks, fume extraction, gas/electrical access and benches for chemical and biological experiments — supports routine sample prep and small-scale synthesis.

Semi-microbalance

A precision balance for measuring very small masses (typically sub-milligram readability) — important for formulation, reagent weighing and analytical work.

Muffle Furnace

A high-temperature box furnace that isolates samples from combustion byproducts — used for ashing, heat treatments, and materials testing up to very high temperatures.

Rotary Vacuum Evaporator (Rotavap)

Used to gently and efficiently remove solvents under reduced pressure while rotating the sample flask — common in chemical synthesis and natural-product work for solvent removal.
